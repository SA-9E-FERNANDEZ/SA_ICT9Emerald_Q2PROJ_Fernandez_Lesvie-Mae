Sinigang na Baboy

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb pork belly or pork ribs (cut into pieces)
  • 1 onion (quartered)
  • 2 medium tomatoes (quartered)
  • 1 radish (sliced)
  • 1 eggplant (sliced)
  • 1 cup green beans (cut into 2-inch pieces)
  • 1 cup kangkong (water spinach) or other greens
  • 2-3 long green chili peppers (siling pang-sigang)
  • 1 pack of sinigang mix (or fresh tamarind if available)
  • 6 cups water
  • Fish sauce or salt to taste
  • 1 tbsp cooking oil

Instructions:

1. In a large pot, heat cooking oil and sauté onions and tomatoes for 2-3 minutes until softened.

2. Add the pork pieces and cook until browned.

3. Pour in water and bring to a boil. Lower the heat and let it simmer for about 40 minutes or until the pork becomes tender.

4. Add the radish, eggplant, and green beans. Cook for another 10 minutes.

5. Add the sinigang mix (or fresh tamarind) and green chili peppers. Stir well and simmer for another 5-10 minutes.

6. Season with fish sauce or salt to taste.

7. Add kangkong and cook for another minute. Remove from heat.

8. Serve hot with steamed rice.

Pancit Canton

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz Pancit Canton noodles (fresh or dried)
  • 1/2 lb chicken breast (sliced thinly)
  • 1/2 lb shrimp (peeled and deveined)
  • 1/2 cup pork (sliced into thin strips)
  • 1 medium carrot (julienned)
  • 1/2 cup cabbage (shredded)
  • 1/4 cup green onions (chopped)
  • 1 onion (sliced)
  • 3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup oyster sauce
  • 1/4 cup chicken broth
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 tbsp cooking oil
  • Lemon or calamansi wedges (for serving)

Instructions:

1. Cook the Pancit Canton noodles according to package instructions, then set them aside, draining any excess water.

2. In a large wok or frying pan, heat cooking oil over medium-high heat. Sauté garlic and onion until fragrant.

3. Add the chicken, pork, and shrimp. Stir-fry for 5-7 minutes until the meat is cooked.

4. Add the carrots and cabbage, and continue stir-frying for another 2-3 minutes.

5. Pour in the soy sauce, oyster sauce, chicken broth, fish sauce, and black pepper. Stir well to combine.

6. Add the cooked noodles to the pan and toss everything together until the noodles are coated with the sauce and the ingredients are evenly distributed.

7. Garnish with chopped green onions and serve with lemon or calamansi wedges on the side.

Adobo

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb chicken or pork (cut into pieces)
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup vinegar
  • 1 medium onion (sliced)
  • 5 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp whole black peppercorns
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar (optional)
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

1. In a bowl, combine soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, onion, bay leaves, peppercorns, and brown sugar (if using). Add the chicken or pork pieces and marinate for at least 30 minutes.

2. In a large pan or pot, heat the cooking oil over medium heat. Add the marinated meat, including the marinade, and bring to a simmer.

3. Add water and stir. Cover and cook for 25-30 minutes or until the meat becomes tender and the sauce thickens.

4. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

5. Serve with steamed white rice.
