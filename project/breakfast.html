Filipino Breakfast Recipes

Tapsilog

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb beef sirloin (sliced thinly)
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1/4 tsp ground pepper
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • 2 cups cooked rice (for sinangag)
  • 2 eggs (for itlog)
  • 1/4 cup chopped green onions (for garnish)

Instructions:

1. Marinate the beef slices in soy sauce, sugar, ground pepper, and minced garlic for at least 30 minutes.

2. Heat cooking oil in a pan and cook the beef slices until browned and tender.

3. In the same pan, sauté cooked rice and garlic for the sinangag, until slightly crispy and fragrant.

4. Fry the eggs sunny-side up or scrambled, as preferred.

5. Serve the tapa, sinangag, and itlog together, garnished with chopped green onions.

Longsilog

Ingredients:

  • 4 pieces longganisa (Filipino sausage)
  • 2 cups cooked rice (for sinangag)
  • 2 eggs (for itlog)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

1. Cook longganisa according to package instructions, typically by frying it until golden brown and cooked through.

2. In a pan, heat cooking oil and sauté garlic until fragrant. Add in the cooked rice and fry for sinangag, stirring occasionally.

3. Fry the eggs sunny-side up or scrambled, as preferred.

4. Serve longganisa, sinangag, and itlog together.

Pandesal with Kesong Puti

Ingredients:

  • 12 pieces pandesal (Filipino bread rolls)
  • 1/2 lb kesong puti (Filipino white cheese)
  • Butter (optional)
  • Sugar (optional)

Instructions:

1. Slice the pandesal in half and lightly toast it in the oven or on a grill.

2. Spread a thin layer of butter on the toasted pandesal if desired.

3. Place slices of kesong puti on top of the pandesal. You can also sprinkle some sugar on top for a sweet touch.

4. Serve the pandesal with kesong puti warm, perfect for breakfast with coffee or hot chocolate.
