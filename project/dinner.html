Kare-Kare (Oxtail and Peanut Stew)

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb oxtail (cut into chunks) or pork hock
  • 1/2 lb tripes (optional)
  • 1/2 lb string beans (cut into 2-inch pieces)
  • 1/2 lb eggplant (sliced)
  • 1/2 lb banana blossoms (optional)
  • 1/2 cup peanut butter (or ground peanuts)
  • 1/4 cup rice flour (optional, for thickening)
  • 2 tbsp shrimp paste (bagoong) for serving
  • 6 cups water
  • 1 onion (sliced)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 tbsp cooking oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

1. In a large pot, boil the oxtail and/or tripes in water for about 1.5-2 hours or until tender. Skim off any scum that rises to the surface.

2. In a separate pan, sauté garlic and onion in oil until fragrant.

3. Add the sautéed garlic and onion into the pot with the boiled meat. Add peanut butter and stir to combine. If you prefer a thicker sauce, dissolve rice flour in a bit of water and add it to the pot.

4. Add the string beans, eggplant, and banana blossoms (if using). Let the vegetables cook for about 10 minutes, or until tender.

5. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

6. Serve hot with shrimp paste (bagoong) on the side and steamed rice.

Bistek Tagalog (Filipino Beef Steak)

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb beef sirloin or flank steak (sliced thinly)
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup calamansi juice (or lemon juice)
  • 2 onions (sliced into rings)
  • 3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1/4 tsp ground pepper
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

1. In a bowl, marinate the beef with soy sauce, calamansi juice, minced garlic, and ground pepper. Let it sit for at least 30 minutes.

2. In a large pan, heat cooking oil over medium-high heat. Sauté the onion rings until they turn soft and slightly caramelized, about 5-7 minutes. Remove and set aside.

3. In the same pan, add the marinated beef and cook until browned on both sides, about 5-7 minutes.

4. Pour the marinade over the beef and bring to a simmer. Cook for an additional 5 minutes, or until the sauce slightly thickens.

5. Season with salt to taste.

6. Top the cooked beef with the caramelized onions.

7. Serve with steamed rice and enjoy!

Laing (Taro Leaves in Coconut Milk)

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups dried taro leaves (or fresh if available)
  • 1 can (400 ml) coconut milk
  • 2 tbsp shrimp paste (bagoong)
  • 3-4 dried chili peppers (optional, for heat)
  • 1/2 lb pork belly (cut into small pieces)
  • 1 onion (sliced)
  • 3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 tbsp cooking oil
  • 1 cup water
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

1. In a pan, heat oil and sauté garlic and onion until fragrant.

2. Add the pork belly and cook until browned and crispy.

3. Add the shrimp paste (bagoong) and dried chili peppers, and cook for another 2 minutes.

4. Pour in the coconut milk and water, bringing the mixture to a simmer. Let it cook for 5 minutes.

5. Add the dried taro leaves (or fresh leaves) and stir well. Cover and simmer for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally to avoid burning. Make sure the taro leaves are tender and cooked through.

6. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

7. Serve hot with steamed rice.
